Rangamati is the administrative headquarter and town of Rangamati Hill District in the Chittagong Hill Tracts of Bangladesh. The town is located at 22°37,60N 92°120E and has an altitude of 14 metres (46 ft). The area of the district is 6116 km2 of which 1292 km2 is riverine and 4825 km2 is under forest vegetation. Rangamati is the only district in Bangladesh with international borders with two countries: India and Myanmar1. Rangamati is the most popular destination in the Chittagong Hill Tracts for Bangladeshi visitors, who come to enjoy the scenic splendour of Kaptai Lake, the countrys largest artificial lake, which was created in 1960 for hydroelectricity2.
